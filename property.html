Understanding Property Crimes

Property crimes are crimes that involve the theft of property or certain forms of damage against the property of another person. Examples of property crimes include burglary, larceny, theft, motor vehicle theft, arson, shoplifting, and vandalism.

Types of Property Crimes

  • Burglary: Entering a building unlawfully with the intent to commit a crime, usually theft.
  • Larceny/Theft: The unlawful taking of someone elseâ€™s property with the intent to permanently deprive them of it.
  • Motor Vehicle Theft: The theft or attempted theft of a motor vehicle.
  • Arson: The criminal act of deliberately setting fire to property.
  • Shoplifting: The theft of goods from a retail establishment.
  • Vandalism: The action involving deliberate destruction or damage to public or private property.
